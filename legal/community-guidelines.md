# ShowShak — Community Guidelines & Repeat-Infringer Policy

**Version:** [VERSION] · **Effective date:** [EFFECTIVE_DATE]
**Status:** Open beta — India only (the website is publicly accessible; clip upload is limited to invited curators)

> These Guidelines explain what's allowed on ShowShak and what happens when
> someone breaks the rules. They form part of, and are enforced under, the Terms
> of Service. They reflect the due-diligence content rules in **Rule 3(1)(b) of
> the IT Rules 2021**.

---

## 1. The spirit of ShowShak

ShowShak is a trust-based, creator-curated discovery layer. Curators recommend
shows and movies they love; viewers discover what to watch next and tap "Watch It"
to go to the streaming platform. Keep it about genuine taste, respect other people,
and only post what you have the right to post.

## 2. Post only content you have the right to post

2.1. Upload only clips you created or for which you hold **all necessary rights,
licences, and permissions** — for the **video and the audio**. You confirm this
every time you publish, through the upload **attestation**.

2.2. **No music library / no "use this song".** During the beta, ShowShak does not
provide a catalogue of copyrighted music to add to clips, and you must not add
music or audio you are not entitled to use. (This protects every curator's
safe-harbour position.)

2.3. **Title-blind by design.** Clips do not display the show/movie title until the
"Watch It" moment — please don't defeat this by putting the title in the clip
itself unnecessarily.

## 3. Prohibited content (Rule 3(1)(b), IT Rules 2021)

Do not upload, post, share, or display content that:

1. **infringes** any patent, trademark, copyright, or other proprietary right, or
   belongs to someone else and to which you have no right;
2. is **defamatory, obscene, pornographic, paedophilic**, or invasive of another's
   privacy (including bodily privacy), insulting/harassing on the basis of gender,
   or racially/ethnically objectionable;
3. is **harmful to a child**;
4. **violates any law** in force;
5. **deceives or misleads** about the origin of a message, or is **patently false
   or misinformation**, or impersonates another person;
6. threatens the **unity, integrity, defence, security, or sovereignty of India**,
   friendly relations with foreign States, or public order, or incites any
   cognisable offence or prevents investigation of any offence, or insults any
   foreign State;
7. contains a **software virus** or any other malicious code;
8. is otherwise **harmful, threatening, abusive, hateful, or violent**.

## 4. Behave well

- No harassment, hate speech, bullying, or threats toward curators or users.
- No spam, scams, fake engagement, or manipulation of fires/views/follows.
- No abuse of the "Watch It", report, or grievance features, including **false
  takedown or counter-notice filings**.
- No scraping, reverse-engineering, or interfering with the service.

## 5. Reporting

If you see content that breaks these Guidelines, report it (or, for copyright, use
the takedown form per the Copyright Policy). For copyright complaints we follow the
36-hour / 21-day process in the Copyright Policy. For other grievances, our
Grievance Officer **acknowledges within 24 hours** and **resolves within 15 days**.

## 6. Enforcement — what we may do

Depending on severity and history, we may:
- remove or disable a clip;
- issue a warning;
- temporarily **suspend** an account; or
- **terminate** an account.

We act on valid notices, court orders, and government notifications, and we may act
to protect users and the integrity of the service.

## 7. Repeat-Infringer Policy

7.1. **Strikes.** A **strike** is recorded against a curator when a copyright
complaint (or other rights complaint) against one of their clips is **substantiated
and actioned** (the clip is removed and not later restored).

7.2. **No strike where the curator is vindicated.** If a complaint is withdrawn or
rejected, or the clip is **restored** (e.g. no court order within the 21-day window,
or the complaint was mistaken), the associated strike is **voided** and does **not**
count.

7.3. **Threshold.** A curator who accumulates **[THRESHOLD] substantiated strikes
within a rolling [WINDOW_DAYS]-day period** is subject to **suspension or
termination** of their account.
  > *Placeholder values — counsel review required.* A common starting posture is
  > **3 strikes within 180 days**, but the exact threshold and window must be set
  > with counsel before launch. These values are defined **only here**.

7.4. **Provable and consistent.** Strikes and termination decisions are derived from
an **append-only moderation audit log**, so outcomes are consistent and provable
after the fact.

7.5. **Serious cases.** A single egregious violation (e.g. a clear, repeated, or
commercial-scale infringement, or illegal content) may lead to immediate
termination, regardless of the strike count.
