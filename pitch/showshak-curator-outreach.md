# ShowShak — Curator Outreach (DM + Email)

> Copy for recruiting the founding curator cohort. Tone is **peer-to-peer founder**,
> not corporate. The whole pitch rests on three honest hooks:
> 1. **A home built for their craft** — a dedicated place for show/movie recommendations,
>    where their taste is the point (not buried in a generic algorithm feed).
> 2. **Founding-curator status** — they're early, they shape the product, they get the spotlight.
> 3. **First in line when we monetise** — a real future upside, framed honestly.
>
> **Hard rule — do NOT promise income.** Monetisation is months away, not today.
> Promising earnings that don't exist yet is how new platforms lose creator trust (and
> it contradicts everything we tell investors). We promise the *home*, the *status*, and
> *first access to monetisation when it comes* — never a number, never "easy money."

---

## The mission hook — "India's own platform" (use when it fits the curator)

A powerful *emotional* reason to join, on top of the practical ones: ShowShak is
**homegrown — built in India, built to go global from India.** For a creator, that's
not "another app" — it's a chance to be a founding voice on a platform that's *ours*,
where the value Indian taste creates stays here instead of flowing to platforms owned
abroad.

Use it as **pride and mission**, in one line — never as a lecture or an economics
pitch. Drop-in line for any DM or email:

> "And honestly — this is India's own platform. Built here, built to go global from
> here. I'd love for you to be one of the founding voices on it."

> **Guardrail:** keep it to the pride/mission feeling. Don't promise it'll "beat" anyone
> or make macro claims about ad money — that's a vision conversation, not a recruiting line.

---

## Who to target (so the copy lands)

- **Hungry, rising tastemakers (≈10k–50k), not 100k+ names.** They have the most to gain, the least to lose, and will move for a brand-new platform. Big names won't.
- People who **already** make show/movie recommendation content (reviews, "what to watch," hidden-gem lists) — we're not asking them to make something new, just to post it where it belongs.
- 18+, India-first.

## The DM (Instagram / X / WhatsApp — short, personal, specific)

Personalise the **first line** every time — reference an actual clip of theirs. Generic = ignored.

> Hey [Name] — your [specific video, e.g. "underrated thrillers" reel] is exactly the kind of recommendation I wish more people saw. 🔥
>
> I'm building **ShowShak** — a platform *just* for people like you: curators who recommend shows and movies. Not another all-purpose feed where film recs get buried — a dedicated home where your taste is the whole point, and viewers tap one button to go watch what you recommend.
>
> We're just launching and I'm hand-picking a small founding group of curators. As one of the first, you'd shape the platform — and you'll be first in line when we roll out creator monetisation.
>
> Mind if I send you a quick link and set you up? Takes 5 minutes and you can post the clips you already make.

## What to say on the "what's in it for me" question (honest answers)

- **"Is there money in it?"** → "Not yet, honestly — we're early. We're building creator monetisation (studios paying to promote titles, matched to your taste) and founding curators are first in line when it launches. The audience you build now is what earns later. I'd rather be straight with you than overpromise."
- **"Why should I post here vs Instagram/YouTube?"** → "Because there your film recs fight with everything else and the algorithm buries them. Here, recommending what to watch *is* the platform — your taste isn't a side dish, it's the main course. And there's a real action: people don't just like it, they go *watch* it."
- **"How much work is it?"** → "None extra — it's the clips you already make. Short, vertical, about a show or movie. I'll set you up myself."
- **"It's brand new with no audience."** → "True — that's the opportunity. Founding curators get the early spotlight and shape the product. You're getting in before the line forms, not after."

## Personalisation checklist (do this every time — it's the whole game)

- [ ] Reference a **specific** piece of their content in line one. Never "I love your content."
- [ ] Use their **name**, match their vibe (casual on IG/X, slightly fuller on email).
- [ ] **One ask, low friction:** "Can I send you the link / set you up?" — not a wall of text.
- [ ] Offer to **do the work for them** (set up the account, a 30-sec demo) — removes the activation barrier.
- [ ] **Never promise earnings or numbers.** "First in line when we monetise" is the ceiling of what you say.
- [ ] DM on the platform where they're **most active**; keep it to a few lines.
- [ ] Follow up **once.** No reply after that = move on to the next curator.

## What NOT to say (guardrails)

- ✗ "You'll easily earn from this" / any income figure or timeline you can't guarantee.
- ✗ "We have lots of users / it's blowing up" — don't fake traction; honesty is your edge with smart creators.
- ✗ Generic mass-blast language ("Dear Creator, we'd like to invite you…").
- ✗ Pressure or fake scarcity beyond the genuine truth (founding spots really are limited — that's enough).
