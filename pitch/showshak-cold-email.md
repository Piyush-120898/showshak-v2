# ShowShak — Investor Cold Email

> A cold email is **not** the pitch — it's a request for 15 minutes, landing in an
> inbox that gets 50 of these a day. Its only job is to create enough specific
> curiosity that they open the deck and reply. Average cold reply rates run ~3.4%;
> the ones that hit 15–25% are short, human, specific, and personalised — never
> bulk-looking. Keep it under ~150 words. One clear ask. No attachments that trip
> spam filters — link the deck (a tracked link if you can, so you see opens).

---

## Subject lines (short wins — 2–5 words, lowercase, human)

Pick one. Personalise where you can — a named, specific subject lifts opens ~26%.

1. `the trust layer for what to watch`
2. `India's "what to watch" problem`
3. `built the JustWatch + Letterboxd gap`
4. `pre-seed: trust-based streaming discovery (India)`
5. *(warm/personalised)* `[Firm]'s consumer thesis + ShowShak`

> Avoid: "Investment opportunity," "Seeking funding," "Game-changing app," anything
> that reads like a mass send. Those get deleted on reflex.

## Personalisation checklist (do this for every send — it's the difference)

- [ ] Use their **first name**, never "Dear Investor / Sir."
- [ ] One line on **why them** — a portfolio company, a thesis, a talk they gave, a tweet. Specific, not flattery.
- [ ] Match the **stage** — only email investors who actually write pre-seed/angel cheques in India consumer/media.
- [ ] **Link, don't attach** the deck where possible (tracked link shows you who opened it; raw PDF attachments hurt deliverability). Attach the PDF only if they ask, or if the fund explicitly prefers it.
- [ ] Send **Tue–Thu morning**; avoid Mondays and weekends.
- [ ] Send to **one investor per email** — never a visible group / BCC blast.

## What to send with it

- **Deck:** `ShowShak-Pitch-Deck.pdf` (13 slides) — or a tracked link to it.
- **Live app link** — your single strongest asset; many investors will click the product before the deck.
- Keep the email itself attachment-free if you can; let the links carry it.
