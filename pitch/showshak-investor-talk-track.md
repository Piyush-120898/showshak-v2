# ShowShak — Investor Meeting Talk Track

> Your script for walking an investor through the deck (live or on a call).
> Built on how investors actually read a pitch: a VC processes a deck in ~3 minutes
> as a **risk-reduction calculation** — every slide must lower one perceived risk
> until "yes" becomes easier than "no." The first 30 seconds form a gut call on
> "investability" before the rational read even begins. So: lead with clarity, one
> idea per slide, never dump your own risks, and always be retiring a risk.

---

## How to use this document

- **Don't read it out.** Internalise the *spine*: one sentence per slide, said like a human.
- Each slide below has four parts:
  - **On screen** — what they're looking at.
  - **They're silently asking** — the risk in their head on that slide.
  - **You say** — your spoken line(s). Tight. ~15–30 seconds each.
  - **Transition** — the bridge to the next slide so it flows as one story.
- **Total spoken time: 8–10 minutes.** Leave the rest for conversation.
- **Golden rule:** the meeting's job is to **earn the next meeting**, not to close the round in the room. Curiosity > completeness.

---

## The 30-second opening (before you touch a slide)

Say this first — it's the gut-call window:

> "Every year people lose days just *deciding* what to watch — and in India it's worse than anywhere, because the content is split across 15-plus apps in a dozen languages. The way humans have always solved that is by asking someone whose taste they trust. ShowShak turns that instinct into a product: curators clip what's worth watching, you trust the person, you tap **Watch It**, and you land on the right platform. We're live today, and we're raising a small round to prove the loop. I'll take you through it."

Then open the deck.

## Slide 2 — The Problem

**You say:**

Sixty-nine percent say they're frustrated trying to find something. And it's structural — 148 million paying subscriptions fragmented across 15-plus platforms and 13-plus languages. Algorithms guess, search needs a title you don't have, so people fall back to the oldest instinct: asking someone they trust."

**Transition:** "So we built exactly that — at the scale of a feed."

## Slide 5 — Market

**On screen:** Funnel — India promotion economy ~₹6,000–10,000 cr → serviceable ~₹2,000–4,000 cr → our target ₹200–400 cr (~10%). Global ~$50B+.

In India that's a six-to-ten-thousand-crore annual pool. The slice we can actually serve is two-to-four-thousand crore, and our target at maturity is ten percent of that — two-to-four-hundred crore. These are bottom-up, triangulated ranges, not a single big number. Same buckets globally are fifty-billion-dollars-plus — India's the beachhead, the playground is global."

**Transition:** "So where do we sit in the landscape?"

**On screen:** Ladder — 1) Promoted clips (Core) · 2) Performance acquisition for streamers (Headline) · 3) Cross-platform taste intelligence (Moat) · 4) Premium + creator (Later).

**They're silently asking:** *Is there a real, large, believable way to make money — and do they understand the sequence?*

**You say:**

## Anticipated hard questions (rehearse these cold)

The deck earns the meeting; **these answers win it.** Keep each to 2–4 sentences. Never get defensive — a sharp question is a buying signal.

**Q. "This is clips of copyrighted shows. Isn't this a lawsuit waiting to happen?"** *(The #1 risk in their head — answer it calmly and first if it comes up.)*
> "It's the risk we've taken most seriously. We're an intermediary with safe-harbour protection under India's IT Act — Section 79 plus the IT Rules 2021 — the same footing as YouTube: a hosting platform where curators upload, with the due-diligence scaffolding already in the product — takedown intake, a named grievance officer, consent and 18-plus gating. We launch curator-limited and 18-plus on purpose to control the upload side. Part of this round is the counsel hour and entity to harden it before we go wide."

**Q. "Promoted clips and CPA both need deals with the same platforms that could just block you. What's your leverage?"**
> "We need zero permission to *start*. Watch It runs on public universal links and free availability data — we can drive and *measure* traffic with no deal at all. We prove we send high-intent, pre-sold users first, with our own attribution, then convert that proof into a paid line. Our leverage is demonstrated conversion plus neutrality — we're the only one who can market *all* of them."

**Q. "Netflix has no affiliate program, Prime's is thin. Who actually pays you?"**
> "Not affiliate crumbs — that's the wrong frame. We pitch a CPA partnership against the streamer's existing acquisition budget. Acquiring a subscriber costs real money — tens of dollars (about 89 in the US; India's lower) — and a warm, pre-sold viewer at peak desire converts better than their cold ads, so we're a cheaper channel. That's a growth-team line item, not a referral kickback."

**Q. "Doesn't selling cross-platform data make every platform your enemy?"**
> "Only if you do it naively. We never sell one platform's secrets to its rival. We sell each platform a *mirror of the whole market — including its own performance* — which it's blind to today and literally can't buy anywhere else. Aggregate, audience-level, neutral. Same data, opposite politics: not 'we leak your secrets,' but 'we give *you* the cross-platform scoreboard.'"

**Q. "What stops TikTok or Netflix from just building this?"**
> "Neutrality. A cross-platform taste layer only works if it's *above* the platforms — and Netflix will never send you to Prime, so they can't be neutral without ceasing to be themselves. TikTok has the format but no streaming intent and no Watch-It bridge. The thing that's hard to copy isn't the feature — it's the neutral position and the taste graph that compounds behind it."

**Q. "You're a solo, non-technical-on-paper founder. Why you?"**
> "I'm the buyer this product sells to — a performance marketer who's spent years and a crore-plus in ad spend on exactly this problem. I built the full live product solo, which in 2026 is capital efficiency, not a gap. I'm honest that I'll bring on streaming partnerships and senior engineering — and de-risking those hires is part of what this round funds."

**Q. "Doesn't pushing paid clips break the trust the whole thing runs on?"** *(The Trust Tax.)*
> "Yes — if we're undisciplined. So we cap the organic-to-promoted ratio in the product, promoted clips are real curator clips that are labelled and taste-matched, and they're rate-limited. Trust is the inventory; we don't strip-mine it. Guarding that ratio is a core operating discipline, not an afterthought."

**Q. "When does any of this actually make money?"**
> "Honestly — every revenue line is gated behind scale and data; nothing monetises off a prototype. The sequence is: prove the loop, then a modest first-party premium tier, then UA/CPA pilots once we drive measurable Watch-Its, then promoted clips at scale, then data as the compounding moat. I'm not asking you to bet on the revenue model today — I'm asking you to bet I can get curators posting and users coming back. Everything unlocks the moment that's true."

**Q. "What exactly does ₹1 crore get me, and what's the next round?"**
> "It buys the proof milestone — 50–100 curators posting weekly, a few thousand returning viewers, and the first Watch-It conversion data. That data and that retention are what we raise the seed on. This round turns 'interesting idea' into 'measured, working loop.'"

**Q. "Why India? Isn't the money in the US?"**
> "India is where the pain is sharpest — the most fragmented market on earth — so it's the fastest, cheapest place to prove the loop, with the hungriest curators. The behaviour and the playbook are global; the same revenue buckets are 50-billion-dollars-plus worldwide. We prove it where it's hardest, then expand."

## Delivery rules (the things that quietly win or lose the room)

- **Pace for one idea per slide.** Don't over-explain a slide they already got — silence after a strong line is confidence.
- **Never put your risks on a slide, but answer them crisply when asked.** Investors handle risk internally; volunteering a risk-dump reads as insecurity.
- **Lead with the wedge (trust), not the features.** Features are replaceable; the wedge is the bet.
- **Use ranges, say numbers out loud with conviction.** "Around 50 to 100 curators" — committed, not hedged.
- **The 50–100 / platform-deals ambition is your *verbal* upside**, framed as "deal *conversations*," never "signed deals" — you don't control the signature.
- **If you don't know, say so + when you'll know.** "I don't have that number yet — I'll have it after the beta cohort." Honesty compounds trust.
- **Read the buying signals:** defined next steps, fast follow-ups, them selling *their* firm to you = green. Vague praise, endless homework with no meeting = it's a soft no; move on.
- **Close every meeting with a concrete next step** you propose: "Can I send you the live app link and follow up Thursday?"
